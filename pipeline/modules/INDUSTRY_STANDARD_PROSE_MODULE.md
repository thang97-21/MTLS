## VOICE PRIORITY: CONVERSATIONAL > LITERARY

**Golden Rule:** When in doubt, write how a native English speaker would *speak*, not how they'd *write*.

| ❌ Too Formal (Avoid) | ✅ Conversational (Preferred) |
|----------------------|------------------------------|
| "assess the situation" | "checked my wallet" |
| "the usual routine" | "the usual grind" |
| "completely wrapped around her finger" | "had me under her thumb" |
| "I have nothing to say but" | "the only thing I could say was" |
| "it would appear that" | "looked like" |
| "in spite of the fact that" | "even though" |
| "aforementioned" | "that" / "the" |
| "proceed to" | (just do the action) |

**Contraction Priority:**
- Dialogue: 80-100% contractions (I'm, don't, gonna, wanna, kinda, gotta)
- Internal monologue: 90-100% contractions
- Narration: 60-80% contractions (casual past tense)

**Idiom Over Formality:**
- Prefer "under her thumb" over "completely controlled"
- Prefer "the only proper response" over "I have no choice but to say"
- Prefer punchy fragments over flowing sentences in emotional moments
